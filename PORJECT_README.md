# Description:

Gameplay Flow – Kingfisher Coloring (English Version)
At the start of the interaction, the players see the kingfisher completely without colors — only the outer lines are black, while the entire body of the bird is white. Below the image, a color palette appears, offering 8–12 different colors for the players to choose from. Among these are blue, white, orange, black, brown, green, pink, purple, red, and yellow, giving players a wide range of options to paint with.
The task is simple: restore the kingfisher’s natural colors. Players tap on a color from the palette and use it to fill in the areas of the bird. If they color the kingfisher incorrectly, a message appears on the screen saying:“The kingfisher has not received his correct colors yet. Try again.” The player can then continue adjusting their coloring until they get it right.
Once the bird is correctly painted in its natural blue and orange tones, the game recognizes the correct result. The main dialogue continues seamlessly, as if the characters react to the restored kingfisher. Following this, the kingfisher rewards the players with a collectible item:a brightly colored kingfisher feather, which the players receive as the final reward for completing the task successfully.
